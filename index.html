What is a domain? And hosting? Do I need them?

Hosting is just the place your website and files live
It's basically a computer configured and built to let the internet see the files it has
So yes, you need hosting but a lot of the site builders mentioned later provide this and you're good.

If you DO need hosting

Here are some recommendations:

  • A Small Orange
  • bluehost
  • GreenGeeks

Cheapest plan is almost always just fine. Should not be over $10 per month and more like $5 or less.

And about domains

A domain is just your sites address or URL.
It's like your home address, people could get to your place with direction but it's super useful having addresses.
So yeah, get one of these too.

More about domains

#1 rule here is if you want one and it's available, buy it right now.

Second rule is make sure you renew it.

Last thing, should be shortish and easy to tell someone.

Oh and .com/.net/.org should be ~$10 per year

Domain registrars

A lot of site building services or hosting providers will register a domain for you.

Usually fine, but big recommendation for namecheap.

Pro tips for sites

Keep it simple

Your site should be a platform to show-off awesome work first and foremost. Think "The Crystal Goblet"

Everyone on the internet has ADHD

Less stuff in the way of seeing work the better.

Depth

  • Relevant bits on the surface
  • Reward those who dig deeper

3 Questions

  • What do you do?
  • Who are you?
  • How do I contact you?

What do you do?

  • Portfolio
  • Résumé or CV
  • News / Blog

Who are you?

  • Short bio and maybe a photo
  • Artist's statement

How do I Portfolio?

  • Big, clear and impactful images
  • Have enough images per piece but not too many (best judgement / 8-12)
  • Keep descriptions concise
  • Have a justification for everything

Learn to edit

If you can't decide whether or not to include something... don't.

Also please spell check

News / Blogs

Keep it current or don't keep it at all

How do I contact you?

  • Make it easy
  • Email address somewhere on every page
  • Contact page with any more info
  • Personally not a contact form fan but use judgement
